The frequency peak that corresponds to the lowest frequency (the fundamental component) of the output signal in Figure 7 is 2 Hz. 
The lowest frequency is not the same as the frequency of the input sinusoid because the lowest frequency is related to the Nyquist Frequency.
The Nyquist Frequency is determined by the pulse width of 0.3 sec and is found by:
\begin{equation}
    f = \frac{1}{2 \times T} = \frac{1}{2 \times 0.3 sec} = 1.67 Hz
\end{equation}

Since aliasing occurs at frequencies greater than the Nyquist Frequency, the aliasing in Figure 7 begins at 2 Hz.

The magnitude between Figure 10 and Figure 11 is nearly identical. 
However, for frequencies greater than 4.5 Hz and frequencies less than 0.45 Hz, Figure 10 has a magnitude of 0 while the magnitude in Figure 11 fluctuates.
The sample and hold filter added in Figure 11 holds the signal constant until the next sample time.
The fluctuations in Figure 11 represent the sample period of the filter.
The analytical representation of this phenomena is described as:

\begin{equation}
    p(t) = rect(\frac{t}{T_s} - \frac{1}{2}) = rect(\frac{0.45}{1} - \frac{1}{2}) 
\end{equation}
